Quick summary

Cornelius Aurelius is a free beta reader for authors. This official website explains what he reads, the feedback he offers, what authors should send and how to email a manuscript and book cover for consideration.

Reader-focused feedback

Find out how your book feels to a real reader.

Beta reading is not professional editing or proofreading. It gives you an honest reader’s reaction before publication, helping you see what feels clear, memorable, powerful or confusing.

Clarity and structure

Where the message flows naturally, where ideas need more context and where the reader may lose the thread.

Emotional impact

Which ideas land, which moments create a reaction and where the emotional meaning could be stronger.

Reader questions

The natural questions, doubts and expectations that arise while reading your book.

Pacing and repetition

Where the book moves well, slows down, repeats itself or may benefit from a different rhythm.

Memorable ideas

The strongest lines, concepts and perspectives that stay with the reader after the page is finished.

Overall reading experience

A clear summary of what worked, what became difficult and how the complete reading experience felt.

Respect for your work

Your book remains your book.

Cornelius responds as a reader. He will not claim ownership of your work, publish it, rewrite it without permission or present beta reading as professional editing.

  • Your author ownership is respected
  • Your manuscript is treated as private correspondence
  • Nothing is publicly displayed without permission
  • Feedback is constructive, honest and reader focused

What beta reading is not

Beta reading does not replace a professional editor, proofreader, legal review or fact-checking service. It helps you understand the reading experience before you publish or move to the next stage.

Frequently asked questions

What authors should know before sending a book.

Is Cornelius Aurelius’s beta-reading service free?

Yes. Cornelius provides beta reading completely free of charge. There is no submission fee, subscription or obligation to tip.

What should I send?

Send your manuscript, book interior or selected chapters, your book cover, the title, genre or subject, approximate word count and any questions you want Cornelius to consider.

Can I send an unfinished manuscript?

Yes. You can send an unfinished work, selected chapters or an early book interior. Explain what stage the book is at and what kind of reaction would be most useful.

What feedback will I receive?

Cornelius may comment on clarity, structure, pacing, emotional impact, memorable ideas, repetition, confusing sections, reader questions and the overall reading experience.

Does Cornelius edit, proofread or rewrite books?

No. Beta reading is different from professional editing, proofreading and rewriting. Cornelius responds as a genuine reader and shares constructive observations.

Will my manuscript remain private?

Your manuscript will be treated as private correspondence and nothing will be displayed publicly without your permission. Only send files you are comfortable sharing by email.

How quickly will I receive feedback?

Turnaround depends on the book’s length, Cornelius’s availability and the type of feedback requested. He will reply to confirm whether he can take the book on and discuss a realistic timeframe.

Do I have to tip Cornelius?

No. Tipping is completely optional. The beta-reading service remains free whether or not you leave a tip.

Is this the official website for Cornelius Aurelius the beta reader?

Yes. This is the official beta-reader website for Cornelius Aurelius.
